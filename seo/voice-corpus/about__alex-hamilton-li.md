## What Makes Bay Area Roofing and Exteriors Different?

 

The Bay Area has unique building challenges that national contractors often miss. Wildland-Urban Interface fire zones in the Oakland Hills, Berkeley Hills, and Marin require specific Class A fire-rated materials and installation methods that comply with California Chapter 7A standards. Coastal fog in San Francisco and Pacifica accelerates roof wear differently than the inland heat of Walnut Creek and San Ramon — salt-laden marine air corrodes exposed flashing and can halve the life of untreated metal components. Older housing stock in Oakland — roughly 60% built before 1960, according to the U.S. Census Bureau American Community Survey — often needs structural reinforcement before new materials go on. Every Bay Area jurisdiction also has its own permitting requirements, from San Francisco's historic district overlays to Marin County's defensible space inspections. I have worked in all six counties we serve, and our crews know how to handle every condition.
