## Design Context

### Users
Bay Area homeowners across three segments: affluent owners seeking premium quality, price-conscious shoppers comparing options, and anxious first-timers nervous about major exterior projects. The site must serve all three — projecting confidence for the premium buyer, transparency for the value shopper, and safety/guidance for the first-timer. Primary context: researching exterior remodeling (roofing, siding, windows, ADUs) from their phone or laptop, often after a neighbor's recommendation or a Google search.

### Brand Personality
**Trustworthy, Premium, Warm.** Hamilton is a high-end Bay Area contractor that feels approachable and human — never stiff or corporate. The voice is confident but not pushy, knowledgeable but not condescending. Think: the expert friend who happens to run a top-tier contracting business.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** Elevated craft — warm, grounded, and substantial. The palette (forest green, yellow, cream) evokes natural materials and the built environment. Typography is tight: THE BOLD FONT for all headings/display, DM Sans for body, Satoshi for labels. Three fonts, no more.

**Anti-references — the site must NOT feel like:**
- A generic contractor site (stock tool photos, blue/gray palette, cookie-cutter layout)
- An overly corporate enterprise product (stiff, impersonal, form-heavy)
- A cheap/discount operation (bright sale banners, clutter, low-trust signals)
- An over-designed startup (trendy for trend's sake, excessive animation, style over substance)

**Theme:** Light mode only. Cream (#F4F1EB) default background, white for content cards, dark green (#245945) for footer/dark sections.

### Design System

**Colors:**
- Primary Green: #256346 (buttons, CTAs, accents)
- Primary Green Dark: #245945 (footer, dark sections)
- Yellow: #FFDE21 (secondary accent, CTAs, highlights)
- Cream: #F4F1EB (page background)
- Charcoal: #1A1A1A (primary text)

**Fonts:**
- Display + Headings: THE BOLD FONT (hero headlines, section titles, all headings — uppercase)
- Body: DM Sans 17px/1.7 (paragraphs, descriptions)
- Labels: Satoshi 12px/700 uppercase (badges, tags)
- **3 fonts max. No Fraunces, no Instrument Serif, no Poppins, no Inter, no Montserrat, no Playfair, no Oswald.**

**Spacing:**
- Container max-widths: 1200px (default), 1294px (wide), 1329px (navbar)
- Mobile padding: 15px horizontal
- Section padding: 60-135px vertical
- Component gaps: 8-20px

### Design Principles

1. **Trust at first glance.** Every element should reduce anxiety and build confidence — real photos over stock, social proof prominently placed, clear pricing, no hidden anything. The first 3 seconds must say "these people are legitimate."

2. **Warm premium, not cold luxury.** Elevated doesn't mean untouchable. Use the natural palette, generous whitespace, and editorial typography to feel high-end, but keep copy conversational and imagery real. A homeowner should feel welcomed, not intimidated.

3. **Clarity over cleverness.** Every interaction should be obvious. No mystery meat navigation, no ambiguous CTAs, no animations that delay comprehension. The site serves people making a $15k-$80k decision — respect that gravity with clear information architecture.

4. **Substance over decoration.** Every visual element earns its place by communicating something useful. Decorative elements are minimal and purposeful (reinforcing brand, creating breathing room). If removing something doesn't hurt comprehension, remove it.

5. **Mobile-first, always.** Most visitors arrive from their phone. Touch targets, readable type, and fast load times aren't afterthoughts — they're the primary design surface.

6. **Bold, never safe.** Hamilton's brand has edge and personality. Structure and symmetry are essential — but never at the cost of becoming bland, generic, or forgettable. THE BOLD FONT exists for a reason. Big type, confident statements, strong color. Clean AND bold. Don't sand off the personality to play it safe.

### Accessibility
- Target: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance
- Ensure sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text)
- All images require meaningful alt text
- Keyboard navigation support throughout
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations
- Focus states visible on all interactive elements
