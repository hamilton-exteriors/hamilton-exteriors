# Content Quality & E-E-A-T Audit — Hamilton Exteriors

**Overall Content Quality Score: 63 / 100**

---

## Executive Summary

Hamilton Exteriors has a technically competent content foundation: all service pages exceed minimum word counts, structured data is implemented across every page, pricing tables are published with specific Bay Area figures, and CSLB license #1078806 appears in footers. However, the site has three systemic weaknesses that cap its E-E-A-T ceiling. First, there are no named human authors, no individual team member profiles, and no first-person staff voice anywhere on the site — a critical absence for a $15k–$80k purchase category. Second, all five FAQ sets (Roofing, Siding, Windows, Oakland, and by extension other city pages) are identical: the same five generic questions with the same answers, word for word. Google's QRG specifically flags duplicate, templated FAQ content as a trust signal problem. Third, the blog has only three articles, all dated the same day (March 31, 2026), with no named authors, no bylines, and no content beyond teaser-length text in the index.

## Cross-Cutting Issues

### 1. Identical FAQs Across All Pages — HIGH PRIORITY

Every page (Roofing, Siding, Windows, Oakland, and by implication all 29 city pages) uses the same five FAQ questions and identical answer text:
1. How does pricing work — any hidden fees?
2. Will you beat a competitor's quote?
3. How does billing and financing work?
4. How long does a typical project take?
5. What warranties do you offer?

This is the most significant E-E-A-T risk on the site. Google's QRG categorizes pages that "repeat the same FAQ across multiple service or location pages" as low-quality content when the FAQ provides no page-specific value. For FAQ schema to generate rich results, the questions must be relevant to the page's primary topic. A Windows page FAQ asking "How long does a typical project take?" with a generic "roofing takes 3–5 days" answer is contextually misaligned.

**Fix:** Write 5 page-specific FAQ questions for each service page. The roofing page should answer roofing questions. The Oakland page should answer Oakland-specific permitting, HOA, and WUI questions.

### 2. Rating Inconsistency — HIGH PRIORITY

The hero badge reads "rated 5/5 based on 50 reviews" on every page header. The reviews section just below that on the same pages shows "4.8 Rated 4.8/5.0." These two figures cannot both be true. This is not a minor visual issue — quality raters are trained to look for factual inconsistencies as a trustworthiness signal, and users will notice.

**Fix:** Standardize to 4.8 / 50 reviews across all hero badges. The "5/5" figure appears to be a placeholder not updated after real review aggregation was implemented.

### 3. No Windows Pricing Table — MEDIUM PRIORITY

Roofing has a 10-SKU pricing table. Siding has a 7-SKU pricing table. Windows has no pricing table at all. This creates an uneven experience and weakens the windows page's ability to serve price-conscious buyers — one of the three defined target segments. The FAQ page mentions "per unit or linear foot for windows" but gives no reference price.

**Fix:** Add a per-window pricing table (by window type: single hung, double hung, casement, slider, picture) with installed price ranges for the Bay Area.

### 4. No Named Authors or Staff — MEDIUM PRIORITY

No individual at Hamilton Exteriors is named anywhere visible on the site. The "author" meta tag on all pages reads "Hamilton Exteriors" (the company entity). Blog articles have no bylines. The About/Mission section has 41 words with no biographical content.

For the September 2025 QRG's Experience factor, this is a structural gap. Homeowners making a $15k–$80k decision want to know who they are hiring. A named owner, crew lead, or project manager with credentials and a photo is one of the highest-ROI trust improvements available for this site.

### 5. Blog Content Velocity — LOW PRIORITY (escalates over time)

Three articles from a single publishing date signals a batch content drop rather than an ongoing editorial investment. Google's helpful content evaluation rewards sites that demonstrate consistent, long-term investment in quality content. Publishing 20+ articles over 12 months (at 2/month) and covering all service lines (siding, windows, ADUs) would materially improve the blog's authority contribution.

## Priority Recommendations

### P1 — Fix the rating inconsistency (1 hour)
Change all hero badge text from "rated 5/5 based on 50 reviews" to "rated 4.8/5 based on 50 reviews." This is visible on every page and will be caught by any quality rater.

### P2 — Write page-specific FAQ sets (2–3 days)
Replace the five generic FAQs on each service and city page with questions specific to that topic. Roofing page: questions about shingle types, Bay Area permit timelines, fire zone requirements. Windows page: questions about energy rebates, Title 24, permit requirements. Oakland page: questions about Oakland WUI fire zones, ADU permitting, Alameda County inspections.

### P3 — Add a windows pricing table (half day)
The roofing and siding pricing tables are the strongest conversion and trust elements on those pages. Add an equivalent table to the windows page with per-window installed price ranges by type.

### P4 — Add a named author/founder page (1–2 days)
Create a brief team page or at minimum an About section on the homepage with a named principal, photo, years in business, and credentials. Even one sentence per named staff member materially improves E-E-A-T for a high-ticket local service business.

### P5 — Fix the two broken Oakland city link patterns (1 hour)
Update `/service-areas/alameda/hayward` and `/service-areas/alameda/fremont` to `/service-areas/alameda-county-ca/hayward-ca` and `/service-areas/alameda-county-ca/fremont-ca` to match the site's URL pattern.

### P6 — Link blog articles from service pages and homepage (1 hour)
Add a "From Our Blog" section to the Roofing page linking to the cost guide and seasonal timing articles. Add a blog link to the homepage nav or footer featured section. This passes homepage authority to editorial content and improves the blog's ranking potential.

### P7 — Build blog content cadence (ongoing)
Target 2 articles per month across all service lines. Prioritize: siding cost guide, window replacement cost guide, ADU cost guide, Oakland WUI roofing requirements, James Hardie vs. vinyl comparison, energy roof payback calculator. Each article should have a named author byline.
